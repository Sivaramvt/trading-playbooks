Vol Desk: Iron Condor

Strategy: Hedged Iron Condor (Weekly Nifty Index Options - TUESDAY Expiry)

1. Strategy Overview & Mandate

The mandate for this volatility structure is to be **conservative, delta-neutral at entry**, utilizing defined risk spreads to harvest premium in range-bound or moderate-drift environments. Protective legs are mandatory. Our focus remains on controlled risk and integrity of the execution process, not speculative gambling.

Risk Profile: Defined & Hedged

Entry Delta: Neutral (or close to zero)

Target: Weekly Nifty Options

2. Critical Risk Filters (Red Line)

No position is to be initiated if the following conditions are present. Avoidable risk is unacceptable.

  • **High Overnight Gaps:** Unpredictable, large overnight moves increase gap risk exponentially. Stand aside.
  • **Extreme Implied Volatility (IV):** High VIX environments suggest immediate, sharp directional moves are likely.
  • **Confirmed Event Risk:** Avoid initiation before known, high-impact events (e.g., RBI decisions, major earnings for Nifty components).

4. Exit Protocol

SUCCESSFUL EXIT (Take Profit)

  • **Profit Target:** Exit upon reaching a predefined percentage of maximum profit (e.g., 70-80% of credit received). *Timing is likely EOD Monday or early Tuesday morning before final gamma spike.*
  • **On Expiry:** Hold for maximum credit realization if the position remains unthreatened and theta decay is robust.

FAILED EXIT (Mandatory Stop)

  • **Primary Hard Stop:** Exit the entire position if the loss hits **2X the credit received** (e.g., 100 credit, exit at 200 loss).
  • **Structural Integrity Failure:** If the protective **buy legs are breached**, the max defined loss is imminent. **Immediate closure** of the entire spread is required as final safeguard.

5. The Adjustment Matrix

Adjustments are critical risk defense measures. They are **never** for maximizing profit; they are for *preserving capital* and re-centering delta.

Adjustment Timing

  • **After First Hour:** Wait for initial market consolidation.
  • **EOD (End of Day):** Final review and adjustment before close.
  • **Fast Market:** Adjust **15-30 minutes** after the market move is confirmed as furious/aggressive.
Market Movement Scenario Best Conservative Adjustment Execution Timing
Slow drift toward short strike Wait & observe. Roll threatened short spread slightly OTM if trend is confirmed. End of the day if trend is confirmed, or Early-week.
Slow drift near breakeven Move the unthreatened side closer for extra credit (re-center). Mid-week, only if price stabilizes near strike.
Fast move toward short strike Exit the threatened spread OR add a futures/long option hedge to counter delta. As soon as the market moves aggressively.
Fast move breaches breakeven Close losing spread (threatened leg). Roll the unthreatened side closer and reduce overall position size. Exit immediately if the breakeven point is violated.

Operational Tactics (Fast vs. Slow Market)

FAST MARKET PROTOCOL

  • **Priority:** Exit the most dangerous leg (threatened Put/Call) FIRST.
  • Determine if a directional hedge is required for continuation.
  • Only roll strikes if the momentum clearly slows after the initial breakout.
  • If volatility is too high, **reduce position size** rather than attempting complicated defense rolls.

SLOW MARKET PROTOCOL

  • Wait for price action confirmation (Is it a fake move or consolidation?).
  • Adjust only if the move persists. **Do not adjust too soon** on light volume.
  • Roll strikes only slightly (micro-adjustments) instead of a full reposition.
