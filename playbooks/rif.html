1. Strategy Principles

The RIF (Ratio Iron Fly) is a highly automated, non-directional carry strategy focused on theta capture over a short duration.

Primary Goal: Theta Carry

Holding Period: 3–5 DTE

Policy: Minimal Adjustments (Exit Only)

2. Greek Profile & Objectives

The ratio wing structure dictates a specific Greek profile optimized for stable P&L over multiple days.

Θ

Theta (Time Decay) - Strongly Positive

The structure maximizes decay capture. The objective is to exit when **40–60%** of the ATM straddle decay is realized.

Γ

Gamma (Acceleration) - Low

The ratio wings dramatically reduce net Gamma, ensuring stable MTM and survival during slow trending markets.

V

Vega (Volatility Risk) - Near-Neutral

The structure is designed for **resilience to IV cooling**. Theta is expected to dominate, providing profit even if the net Vega is slightly long.

3. Position Structure & Payoff

The RIF uses a scalable whole-lot structure to achieve 1.5× wing coverage and wider breakevens.

Standard 2:3 RIF Configuration

Sell 2× ATM CE Sell 2× ATM PE Buy 3× OTM CE (0.8% to 1.3% away) Buy 3× OTM PE (0.8% to 1.3% away)

Payoff Characteristics

  • **Max Profit:** Occurs at the ATM short strikes.
  • **Breakevens:** Extended by the ratio structure, providing a larger profit zone.
  • **Controlled Loss:** Max loss is defined and occurs outside the long wings.

4. Volatility Regime Applicability

The choice of wing distance and overall RIF performance are heavily dependent on the current Implied Volatility Percentile (IVP) and VIX levels.

Low IV Regime (VIX 10–13, IVP < 20)

Stable MTM and low gamma environment. Theta is moderate, but cheap, protective wings (0.7–0.9%) make the RIF reliably profitable for conservative carry (Ratio 2:3).

Normal IV Regime (VIX 13–16, IVP 20–60)

Optimal regime. Strong theta decay and low-to-medium vega impact. Allows for 2–3 day target hits. Use 0.8–1.1% wings (Ratio 2:3).

Elevated IV Regime (VIX 16–20, IVP 60–85)

High theta but increased gamma sensitivity. Requires farther wings (1.0–1.3%) and a stronger ratio (1:2 or 2:4). Best used when IV is stabilizing. Avoid strong trending markets.

High IV Regime (VIX > 20, IVP > 85)

Not suitable for RIF. Gamma/vega risk dominates, leading to unpredictable MTM volatility. Avoid multi-day carry in this zone; prefer aggressive directional or calendar strategies.

Vol Crush Regime (Post-event IV drop)

Most profitable environment. ATM straddle collapses fast (rapid decay capture). The wings retain value early, leading to convex gains.

5. Entry Conditions & Rules

All conditions must be met simultaneously for the automation logic to permit entry.

Entry Condition Requirements

  • **DTE Window:** Between 3 and 5 days.
  • **IV/Volatility:** IVP between 20 and 70, AND VIX < 16.
  • **Trend Neutrality:** RSI(1H) between 40 and 60, AND ADX < 22.
  • **Time Filter:** Entry only between 10:30 AM – 2:00 PM (Avoid first 30 mins).
  • **Event Avoidance:** Avoid entry within 24 hours of major macro announcements (RBI, Fed, Budget, etc.).

6. Exit Management & Protocol

CRITICAL: The strategy uses fixed stop-losses and targets. **No Manual Adjustments are allowed** for directional drift or IV shock. All non-profit exits are treated as closures.

Automated Exit Thresholds

Profit Target Exit: Exit when **40–60%** of ATM straddle decay is captured.
MTM Stop-Loss Exit: Exit if MTM loss reaches **−1.0% to −1.5%** of margin deployed.
Drift Exit: If directional drift exceeds **1.0%**, the position must be closed.
Volatility Exit: Exit if VIX rises **+10% intraday** or **+15% across 2 days**.
Wing Breach Exit: Exit if spot **crosses or closes beyond** a long wing.
Time Exit: Exit when DTE **< 1.5 days** (two days before expiry).

Emergency Actions

Manual intervention should only be used to correct system failures or for immediate, decisive exit.

  • **Force MTM Stop Exit:** Use to immediately close the position if MTM hits SL but the automation fails to trigger.
  • **Manual Close Position:** Use to manually book profit or loss in urgent situations where the automation is compromised.
