Directional Credit Spread

Nifty Weekly Options — Tuesday Expiry

Vol Regime: Stable • Contango • Vol Controlled Trend (Preferred) (ATR: Low • IVP: 32 • Skew: 1.6)
Max Risk per Trade: ≤ 0.5% Capital

01 Executive Summary

A Directional Credit Spread (DCS) is a defined-risk short premium strategy that profits from time decay and disciplined directional bias. This manual documents desk-grade, step-by-step procedures for running Bull Put Credit Spreads (BPCS) and Bear Call Credit Spreads (BCCS) on Nifty weekly options that expire every Tuesday. It covers signal logic, strict volatility filters, trade construction, position sizing, exits, and adjustments.

02 Desk Philosophy & Mandates

  • 01.
    Direction first. Enter credit spreads only when directional model and volatility regime align.
  • 02.
    Defined risk. No naked positions.
  • 03.
    Risk per trade. ≤ 0.5% of desk capital (hard limit).
  • 04.
    Regime-aware. Volatility regime hard filters must pass before any entry.
  • 05.
    Operational discipline. All trades logged with reason codes and checklist completion.

04 Direction Model (Bias Engine)

Directional bias requires at least 2 out of 3 Primary signals plus 1 Secondary confirmation.

Rule Bullish Bearish
H1 RSI RSI > 55 RSI < 45
Daily EMA Price > 20EMA & 50EMA Price < 20EMA & 50EMA
SGX/Pre-open Futures Move > +0.4% Move < -0.4%

Secondary signals: India VIX trajectory, 25Δ skew, ATR regime, macro calendar. Use for confirmation.

05 Volatility Regime Framework

ALL ENTRIES require hard volatility filters to pass.

Vol Controlled Trend (Preferred)

  • • Thresholds: ATR 180–220 | IVP 30–50 | ATM IV Stable.
  • • Characteristics: Directional trend intact with controlled volatility.
  • • Desk Action: ✅ Trade (Full size). Direction + Theta favor strategy.

Vol Crush (Allowed)

  • • Thresholds: IV collapses sharply (≥15% drop) | IVP falling High → Mid.
  • • Characteristics: Event-driven IV collapse; trend forming but not confirmed.
  • • Desk Action: ✅ Trade (70% size). Treat as Vol Controlled Trend upon confirmation.

06 Trade Construction

6.1 Bull Put Credit Spread (BPCS)

  • Sell: Put ~10–12Δ (approx 1SD).
  • Buy: Put 200–300 points lower (protection).
  • Target Credit: ₹15–₹25.
  • POP Target: ~70–85%.

6.2 Bear Call Credit Spread (BCCS)

  • Sell: Call ~10–12Δ.
  • Buy: Call 200–300 points higher.
  • Target Credit: ₹15–₹25.

6.3 Position Sizing

Max loss per trade = (wing width - credit received) × lot size. Ensure max loss ≤ 0.5% of desk capital.

Entry Window: Monday to Tuesday morning. Hard Cut: No new trades after Tuesday 11:00 AM.

08 Exit Rules

8.1 Profit Booking

  • Exit when 50–60% of initial credit is captured.
  • Time-based exit: Monday 3:00 PM (reduce or hedge residual).

8.2 Stop Loss / Invalidation

  • Stop-loss = 1.5× credit or spread reaches 50% of max loss.
  • Mandatory exit if directional signals invalidate (H1 RSI flip, price breaches EMA, etc.).

09 Adjustments

Mild Adverse Move (Manage)

  • Roll Away: Buy back sold leg & sell further OTM (maintain credit/POP).
  • Tighten Deltas: Reduce net delta if direction reverses intraday.

Strong Adverse Move (Fail)

  • Cut & Flip: Close losing spread and open opposite direction if model flips.
  • Prohibited: Do not add naked size to losers. Averaging is prohibited.

IV Spike

  • Widen wings (200→300/400) to reduce Vega exposure if trend intact.

10. Weekly SOP (Checklist)

  • Mon 08:30–10:00: Run daily pre-open checks.
  • By 11:00: Finalize bias & place trades (Last entry cut: Tue 11:00 AM).
  • Daily: Monitor P&L, delta drift, IV movement.
  • Mon 15:00: Book winners >50% credit; reduce exposure.
  • Tue (Expiry): Close residual risk before market close.

11. Risk Management & Governance

  • Max Drawdown Trigger: Stop trading if drawdown >5% of capital; invoke review meeting.
  • Trade Logging: Every trade must include reason codes, checklist snapshot, and regime metrics screenshots.
  • Expected Performance: Target annualized returns: 18–25% in low-vol regimes.
